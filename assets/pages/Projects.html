International Projects

Project Period: 2022

Role in the project: WP leader A2202: Technical Solution/MVP”

Founding Institution: European Commision

Founding Program: EIT Digital

INCLUDE makes our ciyies more inclusive by offering an innovate product for charities and city social services agencies to monitor the increasingly high demand for help from the most disadvantaged population and provide more efficient focused and coordinated intervention The number of charities (“No-profit institutions providing social assistance to disadvantaged people”) is expanding at a growth rate higher than market oriented enterprises (350000 charities in Italy only). Increasing the efficiency of charities' interventions by optimizing resources allocation and offering to charities and city agencies means to monitor the demand for assistance and how it is addressed, improves their coordination and reduces costs for both types of social assistance bodies. Exacerbated by Covid, there is an increasing demand for food, medicine and basic assistance from the disadvantaged population, e.g. the elderly who live alone, homeless persons, families with disabled subjects, the unemployed. Through INCLUDE their needs are better monitored and these people receive assistance in a timelier, more efficient and personalized way, while the city has a more comprehensive up-to-date view of the situation of this population to define more effective social policies INCLUDE product integrates a tool for charities operators that exploits a ML technique to match automatically the requests for help from the population to human resources (volunteers); this tools is integrated with a mobile app for volunteers to be alerted, manage the assigned service, and report interventions; It also provides an info-visualization tool for charity managers based on the charity data about people's requests to monitor and analyse the needs status of the disadvantaged population they served and a a data analysis and visualization tool for city managers.

Project Period: 2021

Role in the project: WP Leader “A2102: Technical Solution/MVP”

Founding Institution: European Commision

Founding Program: EIT Digital

In ageing European societies, the number of elderly people as a percentage of the overall population is constantly increasing, while fewer and fewer people choose to become healthcare professionals. Care institutions and hospitals, therefore, are facing serious staffing shortages and heavy work pressure has proven to be related to poor quality of care and to incidents such as medication errors. The robotics assistants developed as a result of this EIT Digital innovation activity and commercialised by SARA Robotics provide a turnkey hardware and software solution for care institutions and hospitals to improve care recipients' quality of life and provide support to alleviate caregiver-staffing shortages. For instance, the SARA robot can support elderly people suffering from first-stage dementia to perform specific exercises designed to improve their mental and physical fitness and delay entering the second, more acute stage of the illness. The robot can also play games, support music therapy and perform repetitive tasks, such as reminding staff and clients of certain events and checking the client's wellbeing.

Project Period: 2022-2024

Task Leader WP3 “Platform for inclusion”/Task 1: . Multisensory Spaces

Founding Institution: Fondazione Cariplo and Regione Lombardia

Founding Program: Emblematic Program

The project aims to initiate a multi-phase process in which technology becomes an enabling factor for overcoming the many barriers that still, to this day, limit access to an active lifestyle at all ages. ActivE³ therefore targets children and adolescents for whom physical activity, both at school and during free time, is essential for healthy physical and social development; adults, for whom sports practice serves as a means of recreation, socialization, and prevention; and the elderly, to facilitate care, prevention (physical and cognitive), and opportunities for social interaction. ActivE³ aims to enter primary schools so that all children can have the opportunity to experience sports as a playful, educational, and inclusive activity, regardless of their physical or cognitive challenges. This involves the design and validation of new spaces and technologies aimed at breaking down barriers to sports practice, together with the creation of a training process directed at children, families, and teachers to promote health, prevention, and healthy lifestyles within the school system.

Founding Institution: TIM Foundation

Founding Program: Programma TIM “Liberi di Comunicare”

The 5A project involves the use of interactive applications and innovative tools based on smartphones and wearable headsets that integrate Immersive Virtual Reality (IVR), Augmented Reality (AR), and Conversational Agents (CA). The purpose of the project is to enhance the autonomy of individuals with ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) to promote their social inclusion and improve their quality of life. By integrating various scenarios, the 5A applications help individuals with ASD understand the environmental and socio-organizational characteristics of everyday living environments and correctly perform related activities.

Project Period: 2022-2023

Role in the project: Participant “WP 1.3 Interface Development”

Founding Institution: Italian Ministry of Justice

Founding Program: PON- Governance e Capacità istituzionale - Asse I-Azione 1.4.1

Next Generation UPP is a project of the University of Turin - Departments of Law, Computer Science, and Management - in partnership with eleven universities from northwest Italy. It is part of the "Unified Project for the Dissemination of the Office for the Process and the Implementation of Innovative Operational Models in Judicial Offices to Clear the Backlog," promoted by the Ministry of Justice within the framework of the PON Governance and Institutional Capacity 2014-2020 and carried out in synergy with the initiatives foreseen by the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) in support of justice reform. Next Generation UPP aims to contribute to reducing the average duration of proceedings and eliminating judicial backlog. The project seeks to improve the performance of justice in northwest Italy by enhancing the Offices for the Process, experimenting with new collaborative frameworks between universities and judicial offices, and promoting technological innovation. Activities to strengthen the UPPs will be identified, and models for managing incoming case flows and addressing the civil backlog will be tested. A task force will assist judicial offices in implementing these models.

Project Period: 2017-2019

Role in the project: Participant “WP4: Smart Space Platform Development”

Founding Institution: Politecnico di Milano

Founding Program: Polisocial Award 2017

LudoMi is a project co-funded by POLISOCIAL, , Politecnico di Milano's social engagement and responsibility programme. LudoMi aims to create new educational services, in particular for children affected by intellectual disability, based on an innovative technological solution called Magika. Magika transforms any ordinary room in a Magic Room, where lights, projections, music, sounds, aroma, and tangible materials are “smart”, that is to say digitally controllable, programmable, and interactive. Inside the Magic Room, children can play multisensory games and learning activities tailored to the needs of the single child, that are particularly stimulating and involving. LudoMi partners are educational and therapeutically institutions and associations, and Cornaredo municipality (MI), where 2 Magic Rooms have been installed in 2 schools, and a 4-months evaluative experimentation is currently ongoing.
