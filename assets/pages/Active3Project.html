ActivE³ - Everyone, Evrywhere, Everyday

The ActivE³ - Everyone, Everywhere, Everyday project is a large-scale initiative involving several departments and professionals at Politecnico di Milano. It began in the final months of 2022 and was selected and funded with €3.5 million by Fondazione Cariplo and Regione Lombardia. Univerlecco is the project coordinator, and it also involves: Politecnico di Milano - Polo Territoriale di Lecco, Consiglio Nazionale delle Ricerche - Sede di Lecco, IRCCS “Eugenio Medea” - Associazione La Nostra Famiglia, Fondazione Valduce, Ospedale Valduce, Centro di Riabilitazione “Villa Beretta”, IRCCS INRCA di Casatenovo, ASST Lecco, and ATS Brianza.

The ActivE³ project addresses the needs of children and teenagers for whom sport is essential for growth and social interaction in both school and leisure activities, as well as adults for whom sport provides distraction, social connections, and prevention.

As part of the project, our aim was to evolve the Magic Room concept by incorporating a Sensorized Climbing Wall, which was created by developing an extensive sensor network to integrate with the IoT interface of the Magic Room.

HOW DOES IT WORK?
The Smart Climbing Wall

The climbing wall consists of a standard climbing wall support, on which some of the regular hold sockets have been replaced with holes to accommodate the sensors. The climbing wall measures 3.60 x 2.40 meters, providing ample space for children to climb. Safety cushions are positioned around the climbing wall to comply with safety requirements. Motorized screen projectors can cover the climbing wall if needed to prevent distractions for the children.

Each sensor is connected in a chain and linked to a CAN bus, with a standardized socket to attach commercially available holds. A corner node collects the data from each sensor’s strength measurements and then exposes the data through APIs on the local network. A new module in the Magic Room management system calls these APIs, processes the strength signals, dynamically filters the results, and provides aggregated measures for the games.

The technology is largely inherited from the original Magic Room, with the addition of further activities:
  1. Two projectors: one projecting on the front wall (A) and one on the floor (C), separated by a safety cushion (B).
  2. Motion tracking device (D), now powered by a custom solution using Google MediaPipe.
  3. Sensorized Climbing Wall (E).
  4. Bubble emitter (F).
  5. Dolby Surround 5.1 audio system (G).
  6. Control tablet (H).
  7. Smart light system, controllable in intensity and color, fixed on the ceiling (I).
  8. Text-to-Speech system (J).
What was my role?

My role was twofold. On one hand, I was responsible for developing the integration middleware to include the Sensorized Climbing Wall sensor network within the Magic Room architecture. To complete this part of the project, I organized the work of colleagues to determine the necessary modifications to the core system of the existing Sensorized Climbing Wall. Additionally, I coordinated the work of a subteam dedicated to defining the AI algorithm to enhance the multiperson triangulation of full-body tracking, performed using edge computing approaches.

The second part of my role involved developing and supervising activities to showcase the integration of the systems. I developed one activity and supervised a Master's thesis student while he developed another. Finally, I created the control applications for the field study in which we measured the effect of combining climbing and multisensoriality on the social behaviors of the users. The results of the study are reported in a paper currently under revision.

WHAT DIFFERENT ACTIVITIES CAN BE PERFORMED?

To display the potential of the combinated Multisensory Augmented Climbing experience we devised two experiences to be tested:.

Memory Game

This game is a reinterpretation of the traditional Memory board game. One child plays on the floor and selects one of the cards. The second player must climb the climbing wall to reach the cards placed at the top, grab a hold, and maintain their position for a few seconds. If the two selected cards match, they disappear; otherwise, they turn back, and the game continues.

  • Design Role: Lead designer
  • Development Role: Thesis supervisor
Climbing Path Game

This game is inspired by climbing exercises that involve completing paths. One child acts as the Guide and views a map of the climbing wall projected on the floor screen. The map highlights the starting point, target, and prohibited holds. The Guide moves on the floor to show the climber the holds that lie ahead. The other player must climb from the start to the target without using any of the forbidden holds.

  • Design Role: Lead designer
  • Development Role: Full stack programmer
