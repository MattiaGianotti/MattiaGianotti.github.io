Music & Paint

Music & Paint is an innovative evolution of the Magic Room project, focused on generative content. The primary objective of this project is to promote collaboration between individuals with moderate to severe learning disabilities and complex needs, particularly those on the autism spectrum. Additionally, the project aims to provide caregivers with an innovative tool to support their daily activities. Existing literature suggests that music and painting therapies can foster social interaction for autistic individuals. However, these tools often lack flexibility and struggle to maintain attention. Furthermore, the combined effects of these approaches are still not fully understood.

For this project, we developed an initial prototype using the Magic Room tools, and subsequently created a portable version of the smart space. This portable version was designed to make the tool accessible in contexts where it wasn't feasible to establish a dedicated room.

Timeframe of the project: 2023 - 2024

HOW DOES IT WORK?
The Experience

In our application, we used a shared front-facing projection as a digital canvas, allowing two users to play simultaneously. Each user is equipped with a handheld controller that enables them to paint, erase, or move elements on the canvas while interacting with different melodies. Players can engage with the system by clicking on the Internet of Things (IoT)-enabled controller or by moving around the space. The players can also share their creations by combining their drawings or playing each other's melodies.

The system also includes a hint system designed to support users when needed. It silently detects the users' behaviors, identifying repetitive patterns of actions or activities. Based on these observations, it proposes visual and auditory stimuli to suggest new actions, inspire different interactions, or draw attention to the other player's drawing. Additionally, the caregiver can use a tablet to control the experience, assisting users by cleaning up the drawings, changing drawing colors, or triggering specific actions through the hint system.

Further details on the design process can be found in this scientific paper.

The Generation syntax

Each visual element is paired with a corresponding music counterpart, creating a harmonious balance of structure, predictability, and creativity in the final outcome. This approach allows users to predict the results of their actions, which is especially important for autistic individuals, as it provides a sense of organization and control.

The experience is built upon three layers:

  • Sound nodes, represented as paint spots on the canvas.
  • Melodies, represented as lines that may pass over the paint spots.
  • Composition rules that differentiate between:
    1. Temporary melodies (created by quick movements on the canvas).
    2. Normal melodies (created by slower movements).
    3. Loops (melodies that form a closed shape).

The technology is largely inherited from the original Magic Room, with the addition of specifically designed Smart Objects. These are arranged in a slim, portable fashion to ensure adaptability in any care center's room.
  1. Two projectors, one projecting on the front wall (A) and one projecting on the floor (B)
  2. Kinect Motion tracking device (C),
  3. Smart Object for control (D)
  4. Audio system with Dolby Surround 5.1 (E)
  5. Control tablet (F)
  6. Smart light system controllable in intensity and color, fixed on the ceiling (G)
