Main professor and teaching assistant

Bachelor program of Communication Design - Scuola del Design

The evolution of digital technologies and modes of communication and information exchange multiplies the options available for project representation and communication. The Computer Graphics Laboratory integrates the disciplines of communication design, computer science, and representation, addressing the development of artifacts for visual communication with particular attention to the third dimension, in space and time. Topics are covered both theoretically and practically, to build knowledge based on solid conceptual foundations and a design capability aware of the main communication mechanisms. The Applied Informatics module focuses on web communication, addressing themes related to the presentation of Computer Graphics content and the design and development of dynamic and interactive artifacts. The Digital Drawing module covers the transition from two-dimensional representation to the creation of three-dimensional models, analyzing geometries, transformations, lighting, visual rendering of materials, and tools supporting the construction and visualization of 3D models in the digital space, as well as the process of creating synthetic images. The Techniques of Representation module focuses on aspects related to the design of non-linear and interactive narrative structures based on three-dimensional digital artifacts. The course is structured in cycles of lectures, practical exercises applying the acquired knowledge, and tests to assess theoretical understanding.

Accademic years: 2022-23, 2023-24

Master program of Communication Design - Scuola del Design

Design and prototype a digital service or an interactive application applying user experience design, interaction design and user interface design approaches, tools and methodologies and digital technologies. It's a comprehensive and intensive design workshop that combines design and technology, introducing and applying the key tools and methodologies of the user experience design and user centered design. Through lectures and workshops activities and supported by the teaching team, the students will work in teams to design, develop and present an interactive prototype combining software and hardware components (Arduino). The course structure follows the key UX Design process stages:
  • User Centred Design approach and tools: putting people first with user research, personas, customer journey mapping and user scenarios.
  • Concept and service design: problem definition, ideation and conceptualisation starting from the research findings, development and presentation of a digital product/service concept.
  • Detailed design: information architecture, interaction model, wireframes, user interface design for different devices and screens, basic usability evaluation.
  • Digital technologies and prototyping: introduction to web and mobile apps technologies, tangible interaction, Arduino programming and internet of things.
  • Final presentation: Design solution consolidation, prototyping (digital and physical) and presentation with different visualisation techniques.
Each design workshop stage will end with a presentation that will be assessed by the teaching team and will contribute to the final grade. The project brief will be provided at the beginning of the course.This course is part of a cross-disciplinary integrated coordination

Master program of Computer Science and Engineering

Computing devices and digitally enhanced artefacts are ubiquitously present in most human activities and are smoothly integrated in the fabric of our everyday life, making our living space “smart”. Interactive multimedia content is pervasively available in large or small displays; sensors and actuators are embedded in objects, appliances, vehicles, lights, or furniture; digital “extensions of our body” are available through wearable devices like smart watches, wristbands, or virtual/augmented reality headsets such as Google Cardboard, Samsung Gear VR, or Microsoft Hololens. Physical artifacts and digital devices are Internet-connected, and users communicate with them using new interaction paradigms that go beyond “click” or “touch" and include gestures, body movements, speech, or tangible manipulation. This scenario opens up opportunities for new applications in many domain. Still, as the power of technology increases, we face the challenge of how to design the interfaces and interaction modes so that end users can use these technologies in a meaningful way for meaningful tasks. This course helps students to address this challenge, and provides some methodological and technological know-how needed to master the complexity of these new technologies and to develop innovative solutions in this arena. The course also stimulates a thinking “out of the box” attitude and the attention to user needs and requirements, which are crucial to exploit the full potential of novel interctive technologies and to address the related challenges.

Master program of Computer Science and Engineering

The course of Hypermedia applciation aims at providing students with the methodological and technical knowledge and skills needed to develop, in a systematic and efficient way, high quality web-based multi-device hypermedia applications (for desktop computer, tablet, or smart phone). The course focuses on both the technological aspects of web development, and the methodological and software design perspective. On the technological side, the course focuses on Multilayer Web Architectures, Cloud Computing, Web protocol, Multimedia Formats, Web vs Hybrid vs Native Applications, together with more practical knowledge on the most common impleentation technologies for web implemntation (html, js, css, php, vue.js, SQL, nodeJS). On the methodological side It proposes IDM (Interactive Dialogue Model) as a means to define and represent information architecture, navigation, interaction, and presentation features of complex multi-device applications that are truly usable, i.e., easy to use and consistent with users' requirements.
