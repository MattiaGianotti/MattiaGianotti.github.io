My Teaching Experience

Over the year studyplans, I have had the immense privilege and pleasure of being involved in various teaching activities.

During my Ph.D. and research assistantship, I started as a teaching assistant, gaining valuable experience in designing lessons and supporting students as they developed unique projects. These projects often leveraged cutting-edge Human-Computer Interaction technologies available in our laboratory, allowing students to push creative and technical boundaries, and often transformed in Master Thesis projects.

In the past three accademic years, I have taken on the role of a module leader within university courses. This position has taught me how to effectively plan and manage the organizational aspects of higher education teaching. I have dedicated myself to creating engaging, hands-on courses that prioritize the practical aspects of project creation and management over purely theoretical knowledge. Embracing a "Learn by Doing" approach, my aim has always been to make learning an enjoyable and rewarding experience. It brings me great satisfaction to know that my teaching methods and dedication have resonated well with students, as reflected in consistently high scores in their satisfaction questionnaires.

Bachelor program of Communication Design - Scuola del Design

The rapid evolution of digital technologies and modes of communication has expanded the possibilities for project representation and information exchange. The Computer Graphics Laboratory integrates communication design, computer science, and representation disciplines, emphasizing the creation of artifacts for visual communication with a focus on three-dimensionality in space and time. The course combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, fostering a solid conceptual understanding alongside a design capability informed by core communication mechanisms.
  • Applied Informatics Module: Centers on web communication, exploring the presentation of computer graphics content and the design and development of dynamic, interactive artifacts.
  • Digital Drawing Module: Focuses on the transition from 2D to 3D representation, covering geometry, transformations, lighting, material rendering, and tools for constructing and visualizing 3D models. It also addresses the creation of synthetic images.
  • Techniques of Representation Module: Explores the design of non-linear, interactive narrative structures based on three-dimensional digital artifacts.
The course is structured around cycles of lectures, practical exercises, and assessments, ensuring a balance between theoretical understanding and hands-on application.

Master program of Communication Design - Scuola del Design

This course challenges students to design and prototype a digital service or interactive application by applying User Experience (UX) Design, Interaction Design, and User Interface (UI) Design methodologies, tools, and technologies. It is an intensive, hands-on design workshop that merges design principles with technology, guiding students through the key stages of the UX design process. Students will work in teams, supported by the teaching staff, to design, develop, and present an interactive prototype that integrates both software and hardware components (e.g., Arduino). The course follows a structured process:
  • User-Centered Design (UCD) Approach and Tools: Focus on user research to understand needs and contexts. Students will create personas, customer journey maps, and user scenarios, putting people first in the design process.
  • Concept and Service Design: Starting from research findings, students will define problems, ideate solutions, conceptualize, and develop a digital product or service concept.
  • Detailed Design: This stage includes information architecture, interaction models, wireframes, and UI designs optimized for various devices and screen sizes. Basic usability evaluations are also introduced.
  • Digital Technologies and Prototyping: Students will explore web and mobile application development, tangible interaction, Arduino programming, and Internet of Things (IoT) integration.
  • Final Presentation: Teams will consolidate their designs and create both digital and physical prototypes. The project will culminate in a comprehensive presentation employing advanced visualization techniques.
Each stage of the design workshop concludes with a presentation assessed by the teaching team, contributing to the final grade. A project brief will be provided at the course's outset, offering direction and context. This course fosters cross-disciplinary collaboration, integrating technology and design to develop innovative solutions.

Accademic year studyplans: 2020-21, 2021-22, 2022-23

Master program of Computer Science and Engineering

Computing devices and digitally enhanced artifacts are ubiquitous in most human activities, seamlessly integrated into the fabric of our everyday lives, making our living spaces “smart.” Interactive multimedia content is widely available on large and small displays; sensors and actuators are embedded in objects, appliances, vehicles, lights, and furniture. Digital “extensions of our body” are provided through wearable devices such as smartwatches, wristbands, or virtual/augmented reality headsets like Google Cardboard, Samsung Gear VR, and Microsoft Hololens. Physical artifacts and digital devices are connected to the Internet, enabling users to interact with them through new paradigms that go beyond “click” or “touch,” incorporating gestures, body movements, speech, and tangible manipulation. This scenario opens up opportunities for new applications across many domains. However, as the power of technology increases, we face the challenge of designing interfaces and interaction modes that enable end users to engage with these technologies meaningfully and effectively for their intended tasks. This course helps students address these challenges by providing the methodological and technological expertise needed to navigate the complexity of emerging technologies and develop innovative solutions. It also fosters an “out-of-the-box” mindset and an emphasis on user needs and requirements—both critical for harnessing the full potential of novel interactive technologies and tackling the associated challenges.

Master program of Computer Science and Engineering

The course of Hypermedia applciation aims at providing students with the methodological and technical knowledge and skills needed to develop, in a systematic and efficient way, high quality web-based multi-device hypermedia applications (for desktop computer, tablet, or smart phone). The course focuses on both the technological aspects of web development, and the methodological and software design perspective. On the technological side, the course focuses on Multilayer Web Architectures, Cloud Computing, Web protocol, Multimedia Formats, Web vs Hybrid vs Native Applications, together with more practical knowledge on the most common impleentation technologies for web implemntation (html, js, css, php, vue.js, SQL, nodeJS). On the methodological side It proposes IDM (Interactive Dialogue Model) as a means to define and represent information architecture, navigation, interaction, and presentation features of complex multi-device applications that are truly usable, i.e., easy to use and consistent with users' requirements.
